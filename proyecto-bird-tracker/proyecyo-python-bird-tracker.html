Project

This small project consists of visualizing how different individuals of the species Larus fuscus migrate across Europe over time, visiting a diverse number of countries. This was made possible thanks to Movebank, an online database hosted by the Max Planck Institute for Ornithology. It is designed for the study of different migratory species, ensuring their safety, understanding, and protection.

The tracking databases belong to researchers from all over the world who actively collaborate, either by adding information or conducting different studies based on several of these datasets. Movement data from different individuals of the same species helps to understand how they navigate local areas, migrate across different territories, and evolve. To track each of these individuals, various techniques are used such as Radio Telemetry, Argos Doppler, Acoustic telemetry, GPS, etc. In the case of our database, we only contain data obtained via GPS.
